Know your body, be your doctor
We live in an age of instant gratification. Finding quick fixes for every kind of discomfort has become second nature for most of us, whether it’s eating out because there’s no time to cook, scheduling a conference call during a commute or popping a pill to cure everything from a headache to heartburn.
The unfortunate truth is that these methods lead to the onset of real disease which could be obesity, acid reflux, ulcers, diabetes, cholesterol imbalance or high blood pressure, or more commonly, many of these in combination. This leads to more pill popping and more distress, a cycle that eventually proves toxic to body and the mind.
Our ancestors did not have many of these diseases and certainly not in their 20s and 30s as we do now! That is because they understood the importance of eating right, used the power of contemplation and treated their illnesses naturally. They found their remedies in their own organic garden or kitchen, took charge of their bodies and were their own doctors. They waited out the period of healing patiently and knew how to keep daily stress out of their lives.
In that sense they knew intuitively what naturopathy advocates today – that disease originates in the mind and manifests itself through the body.
That is similar to what naturotherapy practioner Sejal Vora advocates today, bringing with her a modern day understanding of nutrition, mind-over-matter wisdom and holistic healing.
